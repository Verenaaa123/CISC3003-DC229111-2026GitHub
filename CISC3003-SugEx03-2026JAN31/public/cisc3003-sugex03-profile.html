Growth mindset

During my internship at Electricidade de Macau, I worked with twelve departments that varied widely in technical background and work style. A major challenge was resistance to a new system, as many team members felt it made their work more complicated rather than easier. Initially, this created tension and slowed collaboration. Instead of pushing technical changes directly, I focused on understanding the human side of the problem. I spoke with users across departments and realized that frustration came from unclear logic and lack of shared understanding, not from unwillingness to cooperate. To address this, I organized a cross-department meeting with IT staff and managers, clarified project goals, explained the logic behind the revised workflow, and encouraged open discussion. By aligning expectations and simplifying processes, collaboration gradually improved. This experience taught me that effective teamwork often requires empathy, communication, and patience—not just technical fixes.
